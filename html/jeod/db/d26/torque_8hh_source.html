68 /**
69  * A Torque represents a Newtonian torque that acts on a DynBody.
70  * The class encapsulates an active flag and a 3-vector that contains the
71  * torque components. Torques are collected in one of a DynBody object's
72  * torque collection STL vectors. The torque vector is expressed in the
73  * structural frame of that DynBody object.
74  *
75  * The Torque class is the recommended mechanism for representing torques in
76  * JEOD. While 3-vectors can also be collected into a collect STL vector,
77  * theee is is no way to turn off these collected 3-vectors. Even worse, there
78  * is no way to tell whether a collected 3-vector does indeed represent a
79  * torque, or even if it is a 3-vector. In comparison, Torque objects can be
80  * turned on and off, and more importantly, they are type-safe.
81  */
